The present tense/El presente

Regular Verbs

In Spanish, verbs have three different endings: ar, er, and ir. Their conjugation is based on the subject of the sentence. This table shows how to conjugate regular verbs in the present tense. If you need to review the subject pronouns, please review this page.
Subject -ar -er -ir
Yo -o -o -o
Tú -as -es -es
Él, Ella, Usted -a -e -e
Nosotros -amos -emos -imos
Vosotros -áis -éis -ís
Ellos, Ellas, Ustedes -an -en -en

For example, the verb hablar means 'to speak'. To say the phrase "I speak", drop the -ar ending from hablar and replace it with "o" according to the table above. Thus, "I speak" becomes "yo hablo."

As another example, all of the forms of comer (to eat) are:
Yo como
Tú comes
Ella come
Nosotros comemos
Vosotros coméis
Ellos comen

Irregular Verbs

There are two main types of irregular verbs: irregular yo forms and stem-changing verbs.

Irregular Yo

The following verbs are only different in the yo form; the conjugations for the other subjects are the same as above. Note that some of these verbs have a spelling change. The following lists some general rules.

  • The yo form ends in -go for verbs that end in -guir
  • The g in the yo form changes to a j for verbs that end in -ger or -gir
  • Hacer, poner, salir, and valer end in -go
  • Verbs that end in -cir and -cer change to -zco

Caer Caigo To fall
Conocer Conozco To know
Dar Doy To give

Escoger Escojo To choose
Exigir Exijo To demand
Extinguir Extingo To extinguish

Stem Changes

Sometimes the stem of the verb changes for all forms except nosotros and vosotros.

  1. -o to -ue
    Some common verbs include:
    • Almorzar - to have lunch
    • Contar - to count
    • Costar - to cost
    • Demostrar - to demonstrate/prove
    • Devolver - to return
    • Doler - to hurt
    • Dormir - to sleep
    • Encontrar - to find
    • LLover - to rain
    • Morir - to die
    • Mostrar - to show
    • Poder - to be able to
    • Probar - to taste/to prove
    • Recordar - to remember
    • Remover - to remove
    • Resolver - to resolve
    • Volar - to fly
  2. -e to -ie
    Some common verbs include:
    • Atender - to attend to
    • Calentar - to warm
    • Cerrar - to close
    • Comenzar - to begin
    • Helar - to freeze
    • Despertarse - to wake up
    • Divertirse - to have fun
    • Empezar - to begin
    • Entender - to understand
    • Gobernar - to govern
    • Mentir - to lie
    • Negar - to deny
    • Nevar - to snow
    • Pensar - to think
    • Perder - to lose
    • Preferir - to prefer
    • Recomendar - to recommend
    • Sentar(se) - to sit
    • Sentir - to feel
    • Sugerir - to suggest
    • Tener - to have
    • Venir - to come

    *The yo form for tener is tengo and the yo form for venir is vengo.
  3. -e to -i
    Some common verbs include:
    • Conseguir - to get
    • Decir - to say/tell
    • Elegir- to elect
    • Medir - to measure
    • Pedir - to ask for
    • Repetir - to repeat
    • Reírse - to laugh
    • Seguir - to follow
    • Servir - to serve
    • Sonreírse - to smile
    • Vestirse - to get dressed

    Several of these words have irregular yo forms.
    • Decir -> Digo
    • Conseguir -> Consigo
    • Elegir -> Elijo
    • Seguir -> Sigo
    • Reírse -> me río (All present tense forms have an accent)
  4. -u to -ue
    Note that the only verb with this stem change is jugar.
    • Jugar - to play
  5. -i to -ie
    There are only two common verbs in this category: adquirir and inquirir.
    • Aadquirir - to acquire
    • Inquirir - to inquire
